Kalutara Vidyalaya is a Buddhist boys' school in Kalutara, Sri Lanka. The college was founded in 1941 by Sir Cyril de Zoysa. It is a national school providing primary and secondary education.

About Us

Kalutara Vidyalaya was established in 1941 by Sir Cyril de Zoysa. Initially a mixed school, it became a boys' school in 1942. During World War II, the Royal Air Force took over the school, but it reopened as a national school in 1998. The school's mission is "To produce balanced personalities who can protect their nation and religion".

Academics

Kalutara Vidyalaya offers education from grades 1 to 13, with streams in Science, Commerce, and Arts for A/L students. The school prides itself on high academic standards, especially in national exams like Grade Five Scholarship, G.C.E O/L, and A/L exams.

Extracurricular Activities

Kalutara Vidyalaya offers a wide range of extracurricular activities, including cricket, football, chess, hockey, karate, and athletics. The school has produced notable athletes such as Thilakaratne Dilshan. There are various clubs and societies, including the Buddhist Society, Science Society, and more.

Notable Alumni

Kalutara Vidyalaya has produced many notable alumni who have excelled in various fields. Some of the distinguished alumni include:

Admission

Admission to Kalutara Vidyalaya is competitive and based on academic merit and other criteria. The school accepts applications for primary and secondary grades, with specific entry requirements for each level.

  • Grade 1 Admission - Based on applications and interviews
  • Grade 6 Admission - Based on Grade Five Scholarship Exam results
  • Grade 12 Admission - Based on G.C.E O/L results
